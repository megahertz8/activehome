## The Home Is the User

A home outlives every person who lives in it. A Victorian terrace built in 1890 has had six families, two world wars, and three energy crises. It's still standing. Its owners came and went. The home endured.

Yet we treat homes like they're frozen in time. An EPC is a snapshot ‚Äî a Polaroid taken once a decade, shoved in a drawer, forgotten. It tells you what a home was, not what it could become.

Evolving Home changes that.

**Every home gets a permanent energy biography.** A living record that begins the day it's built and never ends. Every improvement writes a new chapter. Every owner is a guardian ‚Äî temporary custodian of something that will outlast them.

When you buy a home, you inherit its full story. When you sell, the next owner picks up where you left off. The home's evolution is continuous. You're just its current chapter.

## Why Homes Evolve

A home from 1960 wasn't built wrong ‚Äî it was built for 1960. Insulation standards didn't exist. Double glazing was a luxury. Heat pumps were science fiction.

But 1960 was 66 years ago. The technology moved on. The climate moved on. Energy prices moved on. The home stayed still.

That's the gap. Not bad homes ‚Äî **stuck homes**. 29 million UK homes, 80% rated D or below. Not because they can't improve, but because nobody showed them the path.

Evolving Home is the path.

**The climate changes. So should your home.**

## The Flywheel

This is where it gets powerful.

Every home that improves teaches us something. A Victorian terrace in Bristol that installed external wall insulation generates real data ‚Äî what it cost, what it saved, what went wrong, what went right.

That data (anonymised, aggregated, privacy-preserving) makes our recommendations better for every OTHER Victorian terrace in the country. And every semi-detached. And every 1960s bungalow.

**Federated Learning** makes this work at scale:
- Models train on local data ‚Äî nothing raw ever leaves your home
- Only learnings are shared, never personal information  
- Regional patterns emerge naturally (damp in the West Country, wind in Scotland, solar in the South East)
- Real costs replace estimates. Real savings replace assumptions.

The more homes evolve, the smarter the system gets. The smarter the system gets, the more homes evolve.

This flywheel is the moat. It compounds. Every day the system gets better. Every day it gets harder to replicate.
